Color rules · non-negotiable

  • Default surface alternates white and blue-50. The two-surface rhythm carries the page. No other body fills.
  • Default text on light is navy-900 for headlines, gray-700 (#333) for body. On navy: white headlines, white-at-78% body.
  • Primary CTA is blue-600 background, white text. Never another color. Never a gradient. Never canary.
  • Canary-500 is a brand-recognition exclamation point. Money-in stat (max 1 per page). Logo accent on dark. Never a button fill, never a body link, never a highlighter on prose.
  • The navy → blue gradient is reserved for the hero block and the final CTA section. Maximum two gradient sections per page.
  • Black is forbidden as a section fill. The current GHL funnel page's black + yellow is the templated tell we are deliberately killing.
  • Pure neon, electric cyan, lime, magenta — none. Reads as 2018 funnel, not 2026 AI platform.
  • Canary on the wordmark in light contexts is forbidden. Canary stays on the mark for the dark variant.
  • Claret is reserved for genuine urgency moments. Never as a CTA fill, never on more than one line per page.

Numbers are data — always mono

Every dollar figure, percentage, and time unit on the page goes through Geist Mono. "$72,000," "10 seconds," "12-day DOM" all read as dashboard output, not prose. Inline mono inside a sans paragraph is the most distinctive type move on the page — use it.

Eyebrows do the section work

Every H2 is preceded by a Space Grotesk eyebrow at 12px, 0.12em tracking, blue-600. Always paired with a 24px blue rule. The eyebrow + rule is the section-rhythm device — without it the page reads templated.

All-caps is reserved

Reserved for eyebrows and stat-block labels. Never run a headline in all-caps — that is the GHL funnel tell we are killing. Body copy is sentence-case Inter. Italics are forbidden in body copy.

Constraints

Body lines stay under 70 characters — constrain max-width on text columns. Headlines never exceed 12 words. If you need more, you have two headlines. The page rhythm needs whitespace, not text walls.

03 / Logo system
Wordmark · mark · favicon

The accented verb is the brand's most important design decision.

Sentence-case Space Grotesk Semibold. The verb "Scale" carries the brand-recognition color — blue-600 on light surfaces, canary-500 on dark — with "Your Listings" set in navy-900 (or white on dark). The mark is a stylized stepwise climbing line inside a 16px-radius rounded square. References data and growth without going near the house/key/roof clichés that mark every other vendor in the space.

Construction

Clear space & minimum size

Minimum clear space equals the cap-height of the "S" on all sides. The wordmark lockup is fixed: "Scale" carries the accent (blue-600 on light, canary-500 on dark), "Your Listings" sits in navy-900 (or white on dark).

Minimum legible size for the wordmark is 140px wide on screen, 1.5in in print. Below that, switch to the square mark.

The mark's stepwise line is 10px stroke at 200×200 and scales proportionally — never below 2px. Four ascending segments. Dot at the endpoint. No fill on the line.

05 / Voice & tone
Engineer-not-realtor · AI-native SaaS register

Sound like the team that built the thing — not the team that sells it.

Senior PM at Stripe writing a launch post for a real estate vertical product. Or HouseCanary explaining a new analytics endpoint. Confident, plain-spoken, technical when it earns its keep, listing-agent vocabulary throughout. Never the Tom Ferry / Mike Ferry / KW-eXp-REMAX coaching register.

Six principles

01

Direct

Lead with the claim. Proof follows. Never bury the lede.

02

Numbers, not adjectives

"Sub-10-second contact" beats "fast." "$72,000 average commission" beats "high-value."

03

Engineer-not-realtor

The brand is the engineering team, not a coach. Avoid the agent-coaching voice entirely.

04

Listing-agent register

Speak in GCI, MLS, DOM, CMA, BPO, FSBO. Not "real estate" — that's the outsider register.

05

Short sentences

Single ideas per line. Cut the connective tissue. Compound sentences are a smell.

06

Confident, not boastful

State what is true. Let the reader infer the implication. Top producers respect specificity, distrust hype.

FAQ
What top producers ask

Six questions. Plain answers.

What does pay-per-closing actually mean?
You do not pay for leads, contacts, names, or even appointments. You pay when a listing closes and the commission hits. The infrastructure carries the risk because it earns the right to. We measure cost-per-closing against your historical CPL on the strategy call so the math is clear before you commit.
How is this different from kvCORE / BoomTown / FollowUpBoss?
Most legacy real estate platforms are 2018-era CRMs that bolted "AI" features on in late 2023. We built the voice agent, the qualification logic, and the appointment-booking engine ourselves at the model layer. The team is Stanford, Harvard, and MIT engineers from frontier labs. Sub-10-second first contact, average 2:30 from inquiry to booked listing appointment.
Do I need to switch CRMs?
No. We don't replace your CRM, your IDX, or your transaction management. We sit upstream of all of it — capturing inquiries, qualifying with the voice AI, and dropping booked listing appointments into whatever calendar you already use.
How long until I see my first booked listing appointment?
Two weeks from signed agreement to first routed appointment. Week one is intake-flow mapping and AI training on your case-type criteria. Week two is parallel running against your existing intake to calibrate qualification thresholds.
What happens if a listing doesn't close?
You don't pay. There is no monthly retainer, no per-lead fee, no setup charge. The only invoice that hits your books is when a listing closes and the commission has been earned.
Which markets and MLSs do you support?
Primary coverage in the top-50 US MSAs. We onboard one MLS at a time and verify IDX feeds, broker authorization, and TCPA compliance posture for the geography on the strategy call. If we're not a fit for your market, we'll point you to what you should be doing instead.
