## 0. How to use this doc

This bible is the spine. If you are writing copy, designing a page, building a CTA, or styling a component for Scale Your Listings, the answer to "is this on-brand" lives here.

Three rules of thumb in order of priority:

1. **If a section says "do not," that line is non-negotiable.**
2. **If a section says "always," follow it unless there's a documented exception flagged to Neetish.**
3. **If a section says "prefer," lean on judgment.**

Calibrated for one buyer: the listing-side real estate agent or broker doing $5M+ in GCI annually who is exhausted by CRM vendors that pitch the same recycled lead-gen system.

### Positioning statement

For listing-focused real estate agents and brokerages doing $5M to $100M+ in annual gross commission income who are exhausted by recycled lead-gen and CRM-with-AI-bolted-on platforms, **Scale Your Listings** is the AI-native growth engine that delivers seller appointments, qualifies buyer leads, and converts both into closings on a pay-per-closing model. ### Ideal customer profile

**Tier 1 (primary).** Listing agents and small teams doing $5M-$25M GCI annually. Top-25% producers in their MLS. Has a CRM but uses 20% of it. Already runs paid ads on Meta and Google. Has been pitched by at least one of: kvCORE, BoomTown, Sierra, FollowUpBoss in the past 12 months. Decision-maker is the agent themselves or the team lead.

**Tier 2 (secondary).** Mid-size brokerages with 5-50 agents doing $25M-$100M GCI. Decision-maker is the broker/owner or marketing director.

**Tier 3 (do not pursue).** New agents in first 24 months, sub-$2M GCI solo agents, buyer's-agent-only practices, iBuyer ops, real estate investors, FSBO services. Bad fit.

### Anti-customer

If a prospect says any of these on the call, disqualify and refer them out:

- "I just want more buyer leads, not listings"
- "I'm looking for IDX or website hosting"
- "Can you sync this with my Top Producer / Wise Agent / Lion Desk"
- "I'm new and need help with my first deals"

These are CRM-tier buyers. Let kvCORE have them.

## 3. Voice and tone

### Voice principles (always)

1. **Direct.** Lead with the claim. Proof follows.
2. **Numbers, not adjectives.** "Sub-10-second contact" beats "fast." "$72,000 average commission" beats "high-value."
3. **Engineer-not-realtor.** The brand is the engineering team, not a coach. Avoid the agent-coaching voice.
4. **Listing-agent register.** Speak in GCI, MLS, DOM, CMA, BPO. Not "real estate."
5. **Short sentences. Single ideas per line.** Compound sentences are a smell.
6. **Confident. Not boastful.** State what is true.

### Tone register

Senior PM at Stripe writing a launch post for a real estate vertical product. Or HouseCanary explaining a new analytics endpoint. Confident, plain-spoken, technical when it earns its keep.

**Not:** real estate coach (Tom Ferry, Mike Ferry, Brian Buffini), team-recruiting hype, "abundance mindset," any KW-/eXp-/REMAX-style cheerleading register.

### Banned words and phrases

- abundance, mindset, leveraging, synergize, unlock, harness, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, innovative, transform, revolutionary, game-changing
- "Crush it," "scale it," "10X" (despite the brand name — the verb "scale" is the brand mnemonic, not a filler)
- "Build relationships," "high-touch," "white glove," "concierge" (real estate cliché bingo)
- "Massive action," "next-level," "level up"
- Em dashes in body copy. Use periods, commas, colons, or "or."
- Exclamation points in body copy. One per page max.

### Required brand verbs and nouns

- **Scale:** the brand verb. "Scale your listings," "scaled to $40M GCI," "scalable seller pipeline."
- **Listing:** the unit of value. Not "lead," not "deal," not "transaction."
- **Closing:** the payment trigger. "Pay only when listings close."
- **GCI:** the revenue unit. Always uppercase, never spelled out.
- **MLS:** uppercase, never "MLS service" (redundant — that's the M).
- **DOM:** days on market. Insider term, signals you know what an agent's dashboard shows.
- **Sub-10-second:** the speed claim, always digit + hyphen + unit.
- **Engineered:** the tech moat verb.
- **Pay only when:** pricing structure phrasing.

### Voice example

**On-brand:**
> Our voice AI calls every Zestimate inquiry and FSBO ad in under 10 seconds, qualifies for seller intent and motivation, and books a listing appointment directly into your calendar. If a listing does not close, you do not pay.

**Off-brand (do not write):**
> At Scale Your Listings, we believe every top-producing agent deserves cutting-edge AI technology that empowers them to leverage their existing relationships and unlock 10X listing growth.

## 4. Messaging pillars

Five things we say. Every page, ad, and email pulls from this list.

### Pillar 1 — AI-native, not CRM-with-AI-features

Claim: We built the voice agent, the qualification engine, and the seller-nurture orchestration in-house. Legacy CRMs added "AI" in a sprint. We started with AI.

Sample line: *"Most 'AI for real estate' is a ChatGPT key wired into a 2018 CRM. We built the voice agent, the qualification logic, and the appointment-booking engine ourselves."*

### Pillar 2 — Pay only when listings close

Claim: Pay-per-closing pricing. Aligned incentives. You don't pay for leads, contacts, names, or even appointments. You pay when a listing closes and the commission hits.

Sample line: *"You pay nothing for leads, intake, or routing. You pay when listings close. We carry the risk because our infrastructure earns the right to."*

### Pillar 3 — Speed is the product

Claim: Listings are won by the agent who calls the FSBO first. Our infrastructure reaches every new inquiry in under 10 seconds.

Sample line: *"FSBO listings get 12 calls in the first 90 minutes. We make sure ours is the first."*

### Pillar 4 — Engineered, not bolted-on

Claim: A Stanford, Harvard, and MIT engineering team that ships software. Not a real estate veteran with a SaaS license.

Sample line: *"Built by a Stanford, Harvard, and MIT team that ships software, not a real estate coach with a CRM license."*

### Pillar 5 — Listing-agent focused

Claim: We don't pretend to do everything. We win listings and convert buyer leads tied to listing inventory. We don't replace your CRM, your IDX, or your transaction management.

Sample line: *"We do listings. We don't do IDX, we don't do transaction management, we don't do recruiting. We do listings."*

## 6. Typography

### Type stack

```
Display / Headlines:  Space Grotesk (Google Fonts, OFL, free)
Body / UI:            Inter (Google Fonts, OFL, free)
Data / Stats / Mono:  Geist Mono (OFL, Vercel, free)
```

Google Fonts import for the page head:

```html
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Space+Grotesk:wght@400;500;600;700&family=Inter:wght@400;500;600;700&family=Geist+Mono:wght@400;500;600&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

### Why this stack

**Space Grotesk** is HouseCanary's heading font, also used by Linear, Notion's marketing pages, and a wide swath of modern SaaS. Geometric, contemporary, technical without being cold. Signals "built by engineers" while staying friendly enough for an agent-buyer.

**Inter** is the body workhorse. Cleaner readability than HouseCanary's Arial fallback, while sharing the same neutral SaaS register. Used by Stripe, Figma, GitHub, Vercel.

**Geist Mono** for stats, data callouts, and dashboard-feeling moments. We need a monospace because real estate is data-heavy: GCI, DOM, prices, percentages. Inline mono treatment for these numbers signals "we live in the data" without going full developer-tool.

### Typography rules

- **Eyebrows are uppercase Space Grotesk 600 with 0.12em tracking.** This is the section-rhythm device. Always above an H2.
- **Numbers in stat callouts use Geist Mono.** "$72,000," "10 seconds," "12-day DOM" — all read as data, not prose.
- **Headlines never exceed 12 words.** If you need more, you have two headlines.
- **Body lines stay under 70 characters wide.** Constrain max-width on text columns.
- **Italics are forbidden in body copy.** Italics belong to product names if at all (e.g., for an MLS name in a quote).
- **All-caps usage is reserved for eyebrows and stat-block labels.** Never run a headline in all-caps.

## 7. Logo direction (verbal spec, no SVG this round)

### Wordmark

"Scale Your Listings" set in **Space Grotesk Semibold** (600 weight). Letter-spacing -0.02em. The visual emphasis is on the verb "Scale" in `blue-600`, with "Your Listings" in `navy-900`. This mirrors the Sign More Cases pattern (one accented word on the action verb) and creates a brand-family rhyme without copying the cream/Instrument Serif treatment.

```
Scale  Your Listings
^^^^^                (blue-600)
       ^^^^^^^^^^^^^ (navy-900)
```

### Mark / icon

The mark concept is a stylized **upward stepwise line** (think: a line chart climbing right) inside a rounded square. Three to four ascending segments. Set in `blue-600` on white, or `canary-500` on `navy-900` for the dark variant. References the "scale" verb and signals data/growth without being literal (no house icons, no roof shapes — those are kvCORE/BoomTown clichés).

### Don'ts

- No house icon. No keys. No roofs. No "Sold" stickers. Real estate visual clichés are the single fastest way to look like every other vendor in the space.
- Do not use canary-yellow on the wordmark. The accent stays in the mark or accent moments, never in the type.
- Do not animate the logo on hover.
- Minimum clear space around the wordmark equals the cap-height of the "S" on all sides.

When SVG production is queued, this section gets replaced with embedded SVG source like the Sign More Cases bible. For now, the verbal spec is the source of truth.

## 13. Real estate compliance (non-negotiable)

Real estate marketing has more regulatory exposure than dental or PI. Apply these rules to all public surfaces.

### Fair Housing Act

- Never use language, imagery, or targeting that discriminates by protected class (race, color, religion, sex, familial status, national origin, disability, source of income in some jurisdictions).
- Avoid "young professional," "great for families," "executive home," "ideal for newlyweds" — all flagged as Fair Housing risks.
- Never targeting-segment Meta/Google ads by demographic in violation of HUD's 2022 settlement on housing-related ads. Use the housing/credit/employment ad category in Meta's special-ads system.

### MLS rules

- Never republish MLS data without proper IDX feeds and broker authorization.
- Listing photos belong to the listing broker; do not pull from Zillow/Redfin/Realtor.com without permission.
- DOM, list price, and sold price are MLS-controlled data points; cite source ("Per local MLS").

### NAR Code of Ethics

- Article 12: All advertising must be true and not misleading.
- Article 16: No solicitation of currently-listed property except per Standard of Practice 16-2.
- Specific state real estate commissions (TREC, BRE, etc.) have additional ad-disclosure rules: agent name, broker name, broker phone number must appear on most marketing materials.

### TCPA compliance (huge for outbound calling)

- Voice AI making outbound calls must comply with TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) and the 2024 FCC rule extending consent requirements to AI-generated voice.
- Express written consent required before any AI-voice outbound to a prospect's cell phone.
- Robocall registration requirements (Stir/Shaken).
- Penalties are $500-$1,500 per violation. Do not ship outbound voice without compliance review.

### State-specific advertising

- Florida, Texas, California, New York have additional restrictions on real estate solicitation.
- Anti-spam (CAN-SPAM, CASL for Canada) for email outbound.
- "Cold seller calls" to do-not-call list registrants are bar-association-and-state-AG exposed.

### Boilerplate footer disclaimer (use verbatim)

> Scale Your Listings provides marketing and intake infrastructure for real estate agents and brokers. We are not a real estate brokerage and do not list, sell, or buy property. All real estate transactions are conducted by licensed real estate professionals. Performance figures cited reflect representative results from client agents and teams. Individual results vary based on geography, listing inventory, market conditions, and agent execution. Equal Housing Opportunity.

## 15. Quick-reference cheat sheet

If a design LLM needs to recall the system in five lines:

1. **Surface:** alternating white and `blue-50` (`#F2F7FF`). Never canary fills except one accent moment per page.
2. **Text:** `navy-900` (`#20203F`) for headlines, `gray-700` (`#333333`) for body.
3. **Accents:** `blue-600` (`#0A62FF`) for CTAs and links; `canary-500` (`#F7CF46`) sparingly for brand recognition.
4. **Type:** Space Grotesk for display + headings, Inter for body, Geist Mono for stats.
5. **Voice:** engineer-not-realtor. No "abundance," no "10X," no real estate coach register. Specific numbers, short sentences, listing-agent vocabulary.

If unsure on a decision, default to whichever option looks **more like HouseCanary** and **less like kvCORE / BoomTown / Real Geeks**. That heuristic catches 80% of bad calls.
