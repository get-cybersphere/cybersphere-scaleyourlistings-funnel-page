01 / Color
Paper, ink, and one navy accent

Three paper surfaces. Eight ink stops. One chromatic note.

The palette reads editorial print, not Silicon Valley. Default surface is paper-white. Navy is the only chromatic moment on the entire system — reserved for primary CTAs and the rare brand emphasis. The discipline is what makes the aesthetic work.

Logo don'ts

Hard nos
No compass needle

No directional or navigational icon. Compass owns that visual territory and we deliberately do not occupy it.

No real-estate clichés

No house icons, keys, roofs, "for sale" signs. The aesthetic is editorial, not industry-templated.

No color in the logo

No navy, no any chromatic accent inside the mark or wordmark. Pure ink on paper. Always.

No animation

No hover spin, no kinetic effects. The wordmark sits still and says what it says.

No drop shadow

Flat type on flat surface. Editorial register has no place for shadow chrome.

No tightening past −0.022em

The Fraunces italic loses readability when over-tracked. Stay loose.

05 / Voice & tone
Engineer-not-realtor · GCI register

Sound like the team that built the thing — not the team that sells it.

The visual register is editorial. The voice register is technical-engineering. The intentional contrast — visual luxury plus voice precision — is the brand's most distinctive textural choice. Numbers, not adjectives. Plain English, never funnel-speak.

Sounds like usVoice · do
"Sub-10-second contact on every FSBO, expired, and Zestimate inquiry. Pay only when listings close."

Specific. Verbs of action. Numbers carry the weight. The offer is in the sentence, not in a separate "value-prop" bullet.

Doesn't sound like usVoice · don't
"Unlock the power of revolutionary AI to transform your listing pipeline and 10X your business."

Banned-words bingo. No specifics. No verb that means anything. Reads as a templated funnel, exactly the surface we counter-position against.

Sounds like usHeadline · do
"Why this team is different — every lead vendor before us was too slow by design."

Engineer's framing. Names the structural problem. The conclusion is the headline.

Doesn't sound like usHeadline · don't
"Tired of cold leads? It's time to build the dream business you deserve."

Motivational-poster register. "Cold leads" used as adjective. No proof. The reader is being talked at, not addressed.

Sounds like usCaption · do

A real moment with a real number. Time stamps and price stamps. The reader can verify the texture.

08 / Anti-patterns
What we are killing

If unsure, default to Compass. Not GoHighLevel.

Whenever a layout decision is unclear, choose whichever option looks more like a Compass listing page and less like a templated lead-gen funnel. The current SYL landing page already commits this pattern in two places — both flagged below for the next revision.

∅All-caps screaming CTAs

Bad: "BOOK THE STRATEGY CALL → " · "SEE IF YOU QUALIFY TO WORK WITH US"

Good: "Book the strategy call →" — sentence-case, Inter 500, 15px. Compass uses sentence-case throughout. The all-caps button reads as GoHighLevel out of the box.

∅Multi-color treatments

Bad: Gold accent + canary highlight + sage section + claret error chrome on a single page.

Good: Paper, ink, navy. The discipline is the system. The moment a second accent enters, the editorial register collapses and the buyer pattern-matches us to "another templated funnel."

∅Gradient backgrounds

Bad: Hero with a radial-gradient wash, soft-glow CTAs, pill-tag chrome with tinted gradients.

Good: Flat color or photography. Compass uses zero gradients on their site. We follow.

∅Emoji + 👇 funnel arrows

Bad: 👇 pointing at the CTA. 🚀 next to a stat. 🎉 in a success story.

Good: No emoji anywhere in body copy. The visual register is editorial print, not a TikTok caption.

∅Over-rounded chrome

Bad: 24px rounded-corner cards, 999px-radius CTA bars, soft-pill everything.

Good: 4px buttons, 8px cards, 12px largest container. Editorial luxury aesthetics use minimal rounding. Pill tags are the only fully rounded element, used sparingly.

∅Stock-photo agents in suits

Bad: Generic agent-with-clipboard standing in front of a stock home. Hand-shake imagery. Whiteboard-strategy-session.

Good: Real listing photography. Or typography-as-image. Both work; both look unmistakably like our brand.
